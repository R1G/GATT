GATT is a 3D animated space western sci-fi that takes the tone and atmosphere of Marvel's Guardians of the Galaxy and combines it with the structure and themes of Shonen Jump's One Piece. It follows a crew of superhuman bounty hunters hopping from planet to planet in their escape as fugitives under the law, all while liberating those they encounter from the clutches of oppression in whatever form it manifests.

About the Series

A social media app threatens to launch a civil war when it's discovered that criminals use it to siphon funds from the government…

As an entry in the space western genre, GATT blends the chaotic lawlessness of an economy steeped in the bounty system with the sense of discovery granted by the final frontier of space, set amidst the backdrop of escalating civil war tensions. This burgeoning powder keg, spurred on by organized space pirates (forced to live on the outskirts of society) and groups of marginalized citizens (grappling with the inherent distrust of a technocratic, authoritarian regime ruling over vast portions of the known galaxy), becomes the perfect catalyst for human drama to emerge, exploring themes of generational trauma, individualism in the face of suppression, and a greater sense of community: the idea that people need one another, even in an environment that rewards constantly looking over one's shoulder.

The potential for this kind of narrative to inspire real world change and a wider discussion of contemporary issues is the overall goal and purpose of this project.

Pilot Episode

In 2022 we released a 36-minute pilot episode created in Unity Engine.

Switch to Unreal

We are currently remastering our pilot episode and laying the groundwork for future episodes in Unreal Engine 5 because we believe the engine offers more robust tools for real-time film-makers such as ourselves.

About the Team

The team is led by Rigved Deshpande, a former Blizzard technical artist with experience shipping AAA titles. My creative partner, Alfonso, is the only writer on the project, and his near decade of experience with professional screenwriting software has allowed for seamless coordination between us and our team of over 10 voice actors and musicians. This has allowed us to foster an environment for effective collaboration, despite working remotely during the pandemic.

We premiered Episode 1 with a live viewing in virtual reality on the Spatial platform. Over the course of the evening 60+ people tuned in on their phones, PCs and VR headsets, making ours the most popular space that night.

We will give back to the community by releasing the 3D assets we create during production for free. We have a small selection available already on SketchFab. Additionally we will share all the pipeline tools we develop using Python interfacing with the Blender and Unreal APIs on Github.

Our Process

Motion Capture

We use Sony's Mocopi Sensor Kit to record motion capture performances that are streamed directly to our Unreal Metahumans using the Live Link plugin and Take Recorder.

Face Capture

We use a variety of computer-vision techniques to capture authentic facial performances for our characters within Blender and Unreal.

Blender Content Authoring

We author most of our 3D content in Blender because it is free and open-source, encouraging seamless collaboration with the artists we work with.
